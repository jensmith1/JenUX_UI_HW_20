MyMoods

We designed an app to help people be mindful of how cell phone use is affecting their emotions - allowing them to make changes to improve their mental well being. MyMoods sends notifications for users to pay attention to and log their current emotion several times a day. A dynamic graph then shows them what apps they were on at certain times so they can analyze the emotional impact of each app.

1. User Research

Research Plan

We formulated a Research plan then conducted interviews And did a survey also.

Research Graph

We summarized our conclusions from the Interviews and surveys in a fun graph for simple, quick interpretation.

Problem Statement: We observed that users are not mindful about their phone use. This is causing adverse effects, such as guilt and anxiety and other emotional health problems. Users are not aware of specifically what is causing these problems.

2. Definition and Ideation

User Persona

Our User Persona was created from our research analyses.

Storyboard

We follow our User through a journey as she's having unregulated emotions and not even wanting to be social with friends. She becomes mindful of what apps are causing certain emotional responses. This helps her regulate emotions and become happy again.

3. Prototyping

User Flow

A User Flow follows the different User paths our Users will take step by step as they go through onboarding and then receiving notifications and logging emotions. Charts then display her emotions and can be analyzed.

lo-fi prototype

We created a lo-fi prototype to further solve our problem. It was helpful in enabling early visualization of alternative design solutions. his lo-fi prototype also enabled valuable - early User Testing.

4. Testing and Iteration

lo-fi-and-A-B-testing-results

We conducted User Testing on the lo-fi prototype and also did A/B Testing on certain features. This proed very useful to choose the most intuitive design solutions as well as help us iterate on the design.
